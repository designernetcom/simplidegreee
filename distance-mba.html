About Distance MBA

The distance MBA programme deliver a comprehensive curriculum to the students who are unable to attend in-person classes due to various reasons. Students get access to recorded lessons, self-study material, and occasional doubt clearing sessions from the comfort of their own homes. This mode of learning is characterised by its flexibility, enabling working professionals to seamlessly integrate academic pursuits with their career responsibilities. Apart from covering the core areas of business and management, you can choose elective courses to design your degree as per your individual need.

Remote learning has become a popular choice among aspiring candidates because of the countless benefits it has. Let's explore a few of them:

Flexibility and Convenience

There is no rigid schedule or on-campus classes, allowing you to balance work, study, and personal life. You can avoid relocation hassle and commuting, saving much of your valuable time and resources.

Financial Advantages

Distance programmes generally have lower tuition fees compared to traditional MBA programmes. With this, you don't need to quit your job, so there is a constant flow of income. In addition, there are no relocation or campus housing expenses.

Career Advancement

Pursuing an MBA programme helps in upgrading your knowledge base and building your skills as per the current market trends. Acquiring this degree increases your marketability and qualification for leadership roles. In fact, some companies encourage their employees to go for an MBA programme and return to a higher rank in the same company if they qualify.

Personal Growth

Achieve a sense of accomplishment from earning an accredited MBA degree which is highly valuable in the market. Moreover, it is a great opportunity to expand your network on a global scale.

To get enrolled in MBA distance programme, you must fulfill the following eligibility criteria:

Educational Qualification:A candidate should have completed a bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognised university. Minimum marks: Secured at least 50% (45% for reserved categories) in your undergraduate degree.

Work experience (optional):Some universities may prefer candidates with prior work experience.

Entrance Exams:Some government colleges/universities ask for entrance exam score and few may conduct a separate eligibility test.

Choosing the right specialization for your online MSc degree is crucial for your future career success and personal satisfaction. Here are some reasons why it matters:

Focused Academics and Expertise

Specialization allows you to delve deeper into a specific area, gaining extensive knowledge and skills to become an expert in your chosen field. This makes you more competitive and qualified for niche roles compared to graduates with a general MSc degree.

Increased Earning Potential

Specialization often leads to a higher salary package and leadership roles. Professionals with specific expertise and skills are mostly preferred by recruiters for senior-level positions.

Personal Satisfaction

Studying a subject you're passionate about can be more engaging and fulfilling. Choosing a specialization aligns your education with your interests and strengths, leading to a more enjoyable and enriching learning experience.

Networking Opportunities

You can connect with professionals across the globe within your chosen field. This can be valuable for career prospects, industry insights, and potential collaborations.

An MBA opens the door to numerous career opportunities, although the roles and salary will be different in every sector. Some of the career opportunities you get after an MBA degree are as follows:

Job Roles Job Description
Digital Marketing Manager Plans and designs digital marketing campaigns for the company, including social media, SEO/SEM, web, display advertisements, etc. Responsible for integrating digital marketing campaigns from end-to-end.
Finance Manager A professional responsible for the smooth flow of the financial operations of the company and making financial records to monitor the progress. This role involves tasks like cost estimation of projects and monitoring returns.
Human Resources Manager Their role is to lead and direct the HR department, which includes hiring and interviewing staff, overseeing employee salaries, leaves, and ensuring the compliance of company policies.
IT Manager A technical expert who has to ensure the proper functioning of the company's hardware and software while administering system testing and other computer-related updates.
Operations Manager The duty of an Operations Manager relies on the needs and size of the company. The daily responsibilities vary from managing the production of goods and services to planning the distribution of materials.
Supply Chain Manager A skilled professional who is tasked to ensure the smooth and cost-effective flow of production and shipping timeline of the goods. This role involves appropriate usage of software tools like SAP, Oracle, etc.
Business Development Manager This role involves creating sales leads, developing and nurturing relations with customer accounts.
